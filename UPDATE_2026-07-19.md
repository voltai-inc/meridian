# Update — 2026-07-19 · Per-model power differentiation + adjacent racks

**Why.** Jul 17 DC sync: NVIDIA's Global AI Data Center Lead validated the workload-sim gap and described exactly what the industry can't do today — simulations run at a fixed utilization percentage (e.g. 80% load), with **no per-model/per-workload breakdown and no way to differentiate power draw between models or adjacent racks**. The team agreed to strengthen the prototype before engaging NVIDIA's engineering / Sim Ready teams, paired with a clear "here's what we'd do with your data" narrative.

## What changed

### Engine (`power-validation.js`)
- **`TRAIN_MODELS`** — named-model catalog for training power (Llama-3.1-70B/405B dense, gpt-oss-120B, Qwen3-235B, DeepSeek-V3 MoE), mirroring the serving-model catalog.
- **`modelTraceParams(model, cluster)`** — derives the training-trace parameters from the architecture: weak-scaling step time (6 FLOP/param/token over per-GPU effective throughput → the model sets the grid-forcing frequency), checkpoint size (14 B/param mixed-precision Adam state) over a capped storage path (→ dip length), MoE vs dense duty cycle and amplitude (all-to-all exposed vs collectives overlapped). All public-spec-derived estimates; measured telemetry replaces each 1:1.
- **`fleetTrace(cap, jobs)`** — aggregate power of concurrent jobs sharing one site, with per-job traces retained; `trainingTrace` gained a `phase_s` offset so concurrent jobs de-correlate (bit-identical output at `phase_s: 0` — regression-checked).

### Meridian workspace (`site-intelligence.html`, Workload Power section)
- **Training-model selector** — the power trace is now driven by a named model (default Llama-3.1-405B); a DERIVED line shows the audit trail (tokens/step, GPUs, step s, checkpoint TB → s, duty). Editing a derived knob drops to "Custom timing" seeded with the derived values.
- **Per-model comparison strip** — same site, same chip, five models: sparkline, step/forcing frequency (0.03–1.7 Hz spread at the 200 MW demo site), checkpoint signature, peak, ramp, oscillation, sign-off verdict. The point in one table: power draw is a property of the *model*, not the MW number.
- **Adjacent-racks heatmap** — three tenants (selected model 50%, contrasting architecture 30% checkpoint-de-correlated, inference 20%) on one electrical chain; per-rack-group draw over 30 min, plus worst adjacent-rack differential, diversity credit, and the full sign-off bench on the aggregate.
- **Data-provenance panel** — estimated-today → measured-telemetry-ask → verified-label-unlocked, in one row; ESTIMATED badges on all synthesized panels. This is the "what we'd do with your data" narrative, in the product.
